5-Minute Breathing Exercise for Anxiety

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4.8) · 234 reads
Why This Exercise Works

When you're anxious, your breathing becomes shallow and rapid. This exercise helps activate your parasympathetic nervous system, signaling your body that it's safe to relax.

Step-by-Step Guide
Step 1: Find Your Position

Sit comfortably in a chair with your feet flat on the floor, or lie down if you prefer. Place one hand on your chest and the other on your belly.

Step 2: Breathe In (4 seconds)

Slowly breathe in through your nose for 4 seconds. Feel your belly expand into your hand. Your chest should move only slightly.

Step 3: Hold (4 seconds)

Gently hold your breath for 4 seconds. Don't clamp your airway shut - just pause naturally.

Step 4: Breathe Out (6 seconds)

Slowly exhale through your mouth for 6 seconds, making a gentle "whoosh" sound. Feel your belly fall.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue this pattern for 5 minutes. If you lose count, just start again at 1.

💡 Tips for Best Results
  • Practice at the same time each day to build a habit
  • Start with just 2-3 minutes if 5 feels too long
  • Use this exercise at the first sign of anxiety
  • Combine with calming music or nature sounds
🤗 Positive Affirmations
"I am calm and in control" "This feeling will pass" "I have the strength to cope" "My breath anchors me to peace"
